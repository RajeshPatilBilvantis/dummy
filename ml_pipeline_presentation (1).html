Smart List – Cross-sell ML Prediction Pipeline

Objective
Recommend the most likely next product category for clients with exactly single product

Highlights

Data Extraction and Preparation
Model Training
Monthly Pipeline
How it helps in cross-selling

Data Extraction and Preparation – What We Look At

Product purchases the clients have made
When they purchased each product
Their financial profile
Their relationship with us (how long, AUM)
Their demographics

Data Source

dl_tenants_daas.us_wealth_manaement.wealth_management_client_metrics table
Databricks

Organizing Client Information

Categorizing Products: 7 product categories from prod_lob, sub_product_level_1 and sub_product_level_2
Building Client Purchase History
Example: Retirement → Life Insurance → Investments
Focus on product transitions
Only use complete, accurate information
Focus on active clients

Model Training – How the Model Learns

Looks at client patterns, sequences and profiles
Patterns: Clients aged 50-65 with Retirement often need Life Insurance next
Sequences: After Investments, clients frequently add Retirement planning
Profiles: High-asset clients typically expand into Network Products

Model Training – Learning Process

Identifies: When Client A was in this situation, what did they buy next?
Finds common patterns across successful relationships
Learns which factors matter most (age, products, assets, etc.)
The trained model is deployed in Databricks

What Information the Model Uses – Client Purchase History

What products they've bought before
The order they bought them in
How frequently they engage with different types of products

What Information the Model Uses – Client Profile

Age and life stage
Total assets and account values
Investment preferences (stocks, bonds, annuities)
Client segment and relationship tier

Relationship Factors

Preferred channel (advisor, direct, etc.)

How the Model Makes Predictions

Input: Client's complete profile and history
Analysis: Finds patterns to that of similar clients
Output: Recommends most likely next product with confidence score and talking points
Why this works: Based on learned patterns and considers multiple factors simultaneously

Post-predictions

Natural language talking points are generated based on the SHAP analysis for clients
Client demographics are joined to the predictions table

Monthly Pipeline

On the 12th of every month clients with a single product and branchoffice_code 83 are filtered
These records are passed through the trained ML model
Predictions and SHAP-based talking points are generated
Saved in eda_smartlist.us_wealth_management_smartlist.standalone_tp_predictions_single_policy

How This Helps Us Cross-Sell

Recommended Product: What they're most likely to need next based on patterns in the data
Talking Points: What to say to the client
Save Time: No manual analysis needed
Increase Success Rate: Based on patterns
Better Conversations: Data-backed reasons for suggestions
